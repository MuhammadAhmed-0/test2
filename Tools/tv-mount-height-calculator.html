Installation Reality Checklist (So Your Plan Works in Real Life)

Even a perfect height recommendation can fail if installation details are ignored. Use this checklist to avoid common mistakes.

Studs, Wall Types, and Load Safety

  • Find studs: TVs should be anchored into studs whenever possible (common spacing is 16" or 24").
  • Check wall type: Drywall alone is not enough for heavy TVs without proper anchors.
  • Mount rating: Confirm the mount supports your TV‚Äôs weight (and size range).
  • Leveling: Use a level‚Äîsmall tilts look worse on big screens.

Many TVs use VESA mounting patterns. Make sure your mount supports your TV‚Äôs VESA size and that screws/spacers match the TV‚Äôs mounting holes. If you use spacers for a curved back or recessed holes, re-check stability.

  • Leave space for a soundbar so it doesn‚Äôt block the screen or IR receiver.
  • Confirm the TV bottom edge won‚Äôt collide with a console, shelf, or decor.
  • Plan for access to ports (HDMI, power) after mounting.

Cable Management and Power Planning

  • Plan outlet placement behind the TV if possible.
  • Use cable raceways or in-wall rated solutions where appropriate.
  • Leave slack for full-motion mounts that extend away from the wall.

Safety Notes for Homes With Kids and Pets

In family homes, safety is not optional. Wall mounting is often safer than a stand, but only if installed correctly.

  • Anchor the mount properly into studs.
  • Avoid placing climbable furniture under the TV.
  • Keep cords out of reach to reduce pulling risk.
  • Consider a low-profile fixed mount if you want minimal movement.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
