What Is an Aggregate Calculator?

An Aggregate Calculator is a free online tool that helps contractors, builders, landscapers, and homeowners estimate the exact amount of material required for a project. It calculates the volume (m³), weight (kg), and total cost of materials such as gravel, sand, crushed stone, limestone, and recycled concrete based on your input dimensions.

Ordering the wrong amount of aggregate can be expensive. Too little material causes delays, extra delivery charges, and wasted time. Too much material leads to unnecessary costs and leftover waste. This calculator helps you avoid both problems by giving you accurate, instant estimates.

It is commonly used for projects like driveways, patios, foundations, roads, drainage systems, garden paths, shed bases, and landscaping.

Quick Start: Calculate Your Aggregate in Under 1 Minute

  1. Enter your project dimensions (Length × Width × Depth, or Diameter × Depth).
  2. Select the material type or density preset.
  3. Enter your price per cubic meter (optional).
  4. Adjust the waste factor if needed.
  5. Click Calculate Aggregate.

You’ll instantly get the total volume, total weight, and estimated cost—no manual math required.

Aggregate Calculation Formula

This calculator uses standard construction formulas:

  • Rectangular Volume: Length × Width × Depth
  • Circular Volume: π × (Diameter ÷ 2)² × Depth
  • Weight: Volume × Density
  • Total Cost: Volume × Cost per m³

How the Aggregate Calculator Works

Step 1: Enter Your Project Dimensions

First, choose the shape of your project area:

  • Rectangular areas: Driveways, patios, slabs, walkways
  • Circular areas: Fire pits, round patios, garden beds

The calculator uses these values to determine your base volume in cubic meters (m³).

Step 2: Choose Material Type and Density

Each aggregate material has a different density, which affects weight and transport planning. Select a preset or enter a custom density from your supplier.

Material Type Density (kg/m³) Common Uses
Concrete Mix ~2400 Slabs, foundations, structural work
Gravel / Crushed Stone ~1600 Driveways, drainage, base layers
Sand ~1500 Bedding, leveling, patios
Limestone ~1400 Landscaping, sub-base
Recycled Concrete ~1300 Eco-friendly fill, base layers
Expanded Clay ~900 Lightweight landscaping fill

Note: Suppliers may use different names such as pea gravel, base course, road base, sub-base, hardcore, crushed rock. If your supplier provides a specific density, always use that value for best accuracy.

Step 3: Apply Waste and Compaction Factors

In real-world projects, material is lost due to trimming, uneven surfaces, compaction, and handling. That’s why professionals usually order 5–15% extra.

This calculator allows you to apply a waste factor so your final estimate reflects real conditions.

Step 4: Review Your Results

Once you click Calculate Aggregate, you’ll instantly see:

  • Total Volume: m³
  • Total Weight: kg
  • Total Cost: based on your price per m³

Common Uses for This Aggregate Calculator

  • Gravel driveways: base layer and surface layer estimation
  • Patio bases: compacted crushed stone with sand bedding
  • Foundation bases: load-bearing sub-base
  • Drainage trenches: free-draining stone
  • Garden paths: decorative gravel and crushed rock

Metric Units (With Quick Conversions)

This calculator uses metric units. If you work in imperial measurements, use these conversions:

  • 1 cubic yard ≈ 0.7646 m³
  • 1 foot = 0.3048 meters
  • 1 US ton = 907.2 kg

Frequently Asked Questions

How much gravel do I need for my driveway?

Measure the driveway length, width, and depth. Enter those values above, select gravel density, and add a waste factor of 10–15% for best results.

How many cubic meters are in a ton of gravel?

This depends on density. Typical gravel is about 1600 kg/m³, so 1 m³ ≈ 1.6 tons (metric).

How accurate are these results?

The calculator uses standard formulas and average densities. For precise orders, always confirm with your local supplier.

What if my project area is irregular?

Break the area into smaller rectangles or circles, calculate each section separately, and add the totals together.

How much extra material should I order?

Most professionals recommend 5–15% extra depending on the project type.

Can I calculate cost in different currencies?

Yes. Enter your price per cubic meter and select a currency. The calculator will estimate the total.
